# Pre-Camp Video Script — Mike to Players + Parents

**Length:** 4-5 min
**Format:** Loom or podcast-studio talking head
**Audience:** Players watch first, then parents
**Send:** Same week as parent email, before May 7 reply deadline

---

## On-camera notes (top of mind)

- Look at the camera, not the screen.
- Don't apologize for length. Don't stack qualifiers.
- Pause between sections — give them a beat.
- The point is **clarity + conviction**, not polish. A real Mike beats a perfect Mike.

---

## SCRIPT

**[0:00] — Direct address, no intro fluff]**

Hey Stars families. Mike here. Quick video before camp this Saturday in Charlotte — this is the most important meeting we're going to have this season, and I want you ready for it.

**[0:15] — The context]**

I went to a recruiting webinar last week. Three veteran travel coaches — Firecrackers, Lady Dukes, Finesse — talking about how recruiting works in 2026. Most of what they said, I agreed with. One thing I didn't, and I want to be upfront about it.

**[0:35] — Agreement #1: spring is the work]**

What I agreed with: spring is the work. June and July is when coaches show up to events with a list of names — names they already know, video they already watched. They are not window shopping. The work that gets your daughter on that list happens right now. Not at Newberry. Not at the Sparkler. **Now.** Weekly video. Direct emails to specific schools she actually wants to play for. Camps. School research. That's the spring.

If she's not doing that — when summer comes, it's going to be quiet. And quiet is the worst place to be in recruiting.

**[1:15] — Agreement #2: character is what they're watching]**

Second thing I agreed with: once a coach is at your game, the athleticism question is settled. They're not deciding if she can play. They're deciding if she has the **character** to play for them. Body language after a strikeout. How she picks up the dugout. Whether she's the first one out and last one in. Whether she's looking at her parent for approval after every pitch.

I'm going to say something that might be hard to hear — **this includes parents.** If you're carrying her gear through the parking lot, if you're the loudest person in the bleachers correcting umpires, if she looks at you for approval — coaches see that. We're going to talk about this at camp. Not with judgment. Just honestly. Because we're on the same team here.

**[2:15] — The disagreement]**

Now — what I disagreed with.

The webinar said: it's not the club coach's job to get your kid recruited. The player drives the bus. The club is just the assistant.

I think that's how a lot of clubs hide.

Yes — your daughter has to drive. She has to send the emails. She has to do the homework. She has to care more than anyone else. **If she's not in, I cannot help her.** I want you to hear that. I'm not chasing kids who don't want it.

But when she's in — **Stars is in the passenger seat with the map.** That's different from most travel programs. Specifically:

We track which coaches are visiting her profile page. Every link I send a coach has a tracking pixel. I see who's looking, who's coming back, what they're watching. I'm not guessing.

We run a coach CRM. When she has new film, new exit velo, a new big game — I push that update directly to the coaches actively evaluating her. They don't have to remember to come back. **I bring them back.**

When I email a coach about her, it's because I have something specific to say. Not a mass blast. Not a "please look at our team." A targeted update with a tracked link. Because honesty is our currency with college coaches — and the only way I can keep being honest is by being relevant.

**[3:30] — What I need from her]**

Here's what I need from you and your daughter before Saturday.

By Wednesday, I want a reply with five things:
- Her top 10 schools ranked, with a real reason for each
- Last camp she attended
- Last video she posted
- Coaches she's emailed in the last 30 days
- One honest sentence about how the spring is going

You'll get an email with this written down. **Reply to it.** This is what we're going to work through together at camp.

**[4:00] — Close]**

Look — recruiting is a roller coaster. There's going to be turbulence. My job is to be the calm, steady voice telling you we're going to get through it. There is a great fit out there for every player on this roster — and we're going to find it together.

See you Saturday in Charlotte.

— Mike

## Why we're resetting recruiting now

I attended a coach-focused recruiting webinar last week (TheAllianceFP × Sports Recruits, April 28). Three veteran club directors — Firecrackers, Lady Dukes, Finesse — talked about what's working in recruiting right now. A lot of it I agreed with. **One thing I didn't.**

Here's the short version of what they said, and what Stars is doing about it.

### 1. "Be the butter."
College coaches show up to summer events with a **shopping list** — names they already know, video they've already watched. They are not browsing. The work that gets you on the list happens **right now**, in May, before Newberry in June. Weekly video. Targeted emails to specific schools. Camps. School research.

**What this means for your daughter:** if she's not actively reaching out to schools she's interested in, sending video, and showing up to camps — June and July are going to be quiet. Spring is the work.

### 2. Coaches recruit character.
Once a coach is at your game, the athleticism question is settled — that's why they showed up. They're now watching: how she handles a strikeout, body language in the dugout, eye contact with parents, whether she's the first one out and last one in. **Coaches cross kids off the list for things that have nothing to do with stats.**

This includes parents. If your daughter is looking at you for approval after every pitch, or you're carrying her gear through the parking lot, that gets noticed. We'll talk through this at camp — without judgment, but honestly.

### 3. ".300 is great. That means failing 7 out of 10."
Recruiting decisions are made on the at-bats **after** the strikeout, not the strikeout itself. Coaches are watching how she comes back. We need every player playing free, gap-to-gap, not pressing for a home run on every swing.

### 4. The webinar said: *"It's not the club coach's job to get your kid recruited."* **We disagree.**

Most travel programs say "you drive the bus, we just play tournaments." Not us.

Your daughter still has to drive. She has to send the emails, do the school research, show up to camps, post the video, and care more than anyone else. **If she's not in, I can't help her.**

But when she's in, **Stars is in the passenger seat with the map.** Specifically:

- **We track which coaches visit her profile page.** Every link we send a coach has a tracking pixel. We see when she's being looked at, what they watched, and who's coming back. We stop guessing.
- **We run a coach CRM.** When she has new film or a new measurable, we push the update directly to coaches actively evaluating her. They don't have to remember to come back — we bring them back.
- **We have direct conversations with coaches we know.** Honesty is our currency — we will not oversell your daughter to a coach. That means when we vouch for her, it lands.
- **We help build the school list.** Wide net. D1 through NAIA, JUCO, D2, D3. The right *fit* school exists for every player on this roster — but most families only know the top 25. Our job is to widen that view.

This is what "we take recruiting personally" actually means. Not a slogan.

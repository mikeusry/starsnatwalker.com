## Session Flow

### [0:00 — 0:05] Welcome + frame
- Thank them. Acknowledge the long day on the field.
- Frame: *"This is the most important meeting we'll have this season. Not a pep talk. We're going to do work."*
- Phones away. Players + parents sitting together.

### [0:05 — 0:20] What coaches are actually evaluating
**Anchor concept: Be the butter.**

- Tell the ingredient-list story (Adam Glickman, Firecrackers): coaches show up with a shopping list. They are not browsing. Spring is when you get on the list.
- Tell the .300 hitter story: a great hitter fails 7/10 times. They recruit how you handle the failure.
- The Lexi / Jackie Joseph story (Donny's): kid went 0-for-3 with a strikeout, got the offer. Coach saw fearless swing after the K. The thing she thought was a bad showcase was actually her best at-bat.
- Drive home: **once a coach is at your game, athleticism is settled. They're now watching character.** Body language. Hustle. Eye contact. Dugout behavior. Who carries the gear.

### [0:20 — 0:30] The disagreement — and what we do differently
**Anchor concept: We drive the bus *with* you, not for you.**

- Most travel programs say "you drive, we just play tournaments." We don't.
- **You drive on:** effort, character, communication, sending emails, doing the homework, going to camps.
- **We drive on:** advocacy, school list curation, coach relationships, tracking, follow-up updates.
- Show the live program-match outreach CRM (heat scores, last pixel hit, send/reply log). Show a player profile page with the pixel firing — and explain that the pixel feeds program-match automatically.
- *"Every coach link I send has a tracking pixel. When they look at your page, I see it. When they come back, I see it. When they don't, I see that too — and I follow up."*
- The catch: **"If you're not in, I'm not in. We will match your effort. We will not replace it."**

### [0:30 — 0:45] The honest evaluation talk
**Anchor concept: Cast a wide net. D1-or-bust is dead.**

- 1300+ college softball programs. Most families know the top 25.
- Tell Tori's story: "What's your top school?" — "Texas A&M." — "Why?" — "They were the first school to look at me." That's not a why.
- The "potential" trap: potential means you haven't done it yet. Don't get drunk on rankings.
- We will be honest with you. If we tell a coach you're coachable, you are. That's how we keep the door open with coaches over time.
- Wide net = D1, D2, D3, NAIA, JUCO, in that order of *fit*, not prestige.

### [0:45 — 1:00] NIL / rev-share / portal — what's real, what's noise
**Quick. Don't dwell. Goal: deflate the noise so kids stop chasing rumored money.**

- Post-House settlement, ~100 schools have meaningful money. ~98% of softball recruits, NIL is not a recruiting decision factor.
- 80-90% of rev-share goes to football and basketball. Crumbs left for softball.
- Personal-brand NIL exists for kids with social media reach — that's effort outside the sport.
- Transfer portal has tightened roster spots. Means our digital footprint matters more, not less.
- **Don't pick a school chasing rumored money. Pick the fit.**

### [1:00 — 1:25] Per-player working session
**This is the meat of the meeting.**

Pull each player + parent up one at a time (or break into stations if too many — 4-5 min each):
- Review their reply to the parent email (top 10 schools, last camp, last video, coaches emailed)
- Mark which schools are realistic / stretch / need to discover
- Assign **one concrete homework item** for each player before next tournament
- Add 3-5 of their target coaches to the CRM **live, in front of them** — show them this is real

If 14 players × 4 min = 56 min — too long. Either:
- (a) Run 3 stations in parallel (Mike + 2 assistant coaches)
- (b) Do top 5 priority players publicly, rest in 1:1 follow-ups within 7 days

**Decide which by May 7.** Default: stations.

### [1:25 — 1:30] Close + commitments
- Each player states out loud: **one thing she'll do this week.** (Send 3 emails. Post a video. Research 5 schools. Sign up for a camp.)
- Each parent states: **one thing they will stop doing or start doing.** (Examples: "stop coaching from the bleachers." "Start letting her email coaches herself.")
- Calendar: next tournament is **Show Me The Money, Newberry FL, June 5-8** — we have ~4 weeks. We will check in by email at week 2 and week 4.

## Failure modes to watch for

- **A parent gets defensive about body-language critique.** Stay calm. Reframe: *"I'm not saying you're doing it wrong. I'm telling you what college coaches told us they look for. Decide what to do with that."*
- **A player doesn't have a school list.** Don't shame. Use the time to build one in the room — Stars priority is to widen the view, not test her preparation.
- **A parent challenges the "drive the bus" framing — wants more from Stars.** Lean in. *"Tell me what you need. If it's reasonable, we'll add it. If it's not, I'll be honest about why."*
- **The room runs long.** Cut the NIL section first — it's deflation, not action. The 1:1 stations are the irreplaceable part.
